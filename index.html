Conflict is best solved by breaking it into smaller parts. External conflict (tacticals about what to do/how to do something) and internal conflict (your thoughts about why you labeled something a conflict).

Identity also has an external (physical attributes, roles, titles, achievements) and internal component (your WHO).

Your WHO is defined by:

  • Values — Your guardrails
  • Pillars — Your energy source
  • Ideal Emotion — Your compass
  • Trigger — Your warning signal

Conflict happens when you believe your WHO has been threatened.

In 6 steps, you’ll identify:

Values: Your non-negotiables and how you think the world should act. When you are living in alignment with your Values, they are what help you to achieve your goals.

Pillars: Your natural characteristics that describe who you are as your happiest, most relaxed self. They are your strengths, and when you are in a flow state.

Ideal Emotion: What you want to feel each day. Your emotional compass.

Trigger: The “I’m not ___ enough” story that pulls you off course.

Step 1 of 6: Values (Discover)

Build your Values candidate list

Reflect on what matters to you. What are the non-negotiable rules that drive your success? Tap to select 3–6 of your Values OR add custom ones. We’ll road-test on the next step.

Step 2 of 6: Values Evoke Emotions (Road Test)

Road test each candidate. Values, when crossed, evoke an emotion.

If someone violates this, do you feel upset / angry / frustrated?

YES = it’s a Value (keep)

NO = it’s not a Value (remove from the list)

Practical Application:

Clarity comes by identifying conflicting Values.

Use your Values as guardrails when making tough decisions.

Let them guide your boundaries in relationships and work.

By identifying these candidates, you can more easily de-escalate your emotions.

Step 3 of 6: Pillars (Discover)

Are positive core characteristics that describe you at your best (they are not tied to accomplishment or how you think you "should be"). You are great as you are!

When were you your happiest and most YOU?

Step 4 of 6: Pillars (Road Test)

If someone crosses this characteristic, do you get angry/frustrated/upset?

If you took these characteristics away, would you be a shell of yourself?

Practical Application:

Lead from your unique strengths.

Lean on your Pillars when challenges feel heavy.

Internal Conflict & Choice
